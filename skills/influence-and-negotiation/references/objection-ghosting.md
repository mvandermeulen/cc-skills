# Late-stage stall / ghosting

The buyer goes radio-silent at late stage — between proposal and signature, or after a redline cycle. No reply for 10–14 days.

The instinct: chase with concessions to break the silence. The instinct is wrong — the silence may be tactical (pressure for concessions) or genuine (internal change), and concession-chasing rewards the tactical version.

**Diagnostic sequence:**

1. **Calibrated re-engagement** — Day 7: a single short email re-stating the value summary, no concession. _"Wanted to keep the value summary fresh in mind as the team works through the internal review — happy to support if a question comes up."_
2. **Negative reverse** — Day 12–14, if no reply: see [Negative reverse](objection-procurement-playbook.md#negative-reverse--for-ghosted-deals).
3. **Champion check** — Day 17, if negative reverse produced no reply: contact the champion via a different channel (LinkedIn DM, mutual connection). The champion's silence is the signal — usually means internal change you weren't told about.
4. **EB escalation** — Day 21+, if you've earned the right (see [references/playbook-eb-engagement.md](playbook-eb-engagement.md)): contact the EB directly with a 1-paragraph status note. Last resort.

**Anti-pattern.** Sending repeated soft chase emails ("just checking in," "circling back") teaches the buyer that silence is rewarded with continued seller attention. Ladder cleanly and stop.
