# Champion test — is your "champion" actually a coach?

**Trigger.** You've had 2–3 calls with a contact who feels supportive. Your CRM has them flagged as Champion. Time to verify before the deal moves to validation.

**The three observable behaviours of a real champion:**

1. **Proactive coordination.** They surface internal context you didn't ask for. _"By the way, the CFO is reviewing all software spend over $50K starting next month."_ A coach reacts; a champion volunteers.
2. **Information you didn't request.** They share the political map, the previous vendor's sins, who really decides what. They do this because your win is their win.
3. **Action between calls.** Coach: passive between meetings. Champion: actively pushing internally — sending your one-pager to the CFO, scheduling the security review, blocking time on the EB's calendar for you.

**The 3-question commitment test** (see [references/discovery.md](discovery.md#champion-validation--the-3-question-commitment-test)):

1. _"How will we know we're on target?"_
2. _"What will we do if we're not?"_
3. _"How are we going to close this?"_

A real champion answers each with specifics and named people. A coach generalises, hedges, or pivots.

**If your champion fails the test.** They might be a coach (helpful but no political capital), an influencer (some capital but not enough), or a saboteur (working against you under the surface). Three options:

- **Build their capital.** Arm them with a one-pager + ROI calculator + objection-FAQ. Often their issue is not motivation but ammunition.
- **Find a parallel champion.** Multi-thread without burning the current contact. _"It would help me to validate this with X — would you be open to a brief intro?"_
- **Re-qualify the deal.** No champion = MEDDPICC failure. Lower the deal stage in CRM until the gap is closed.

**Failure signal.** Three calls in, you can't name the champion's enjeu (their personal stake). They're a coach.
