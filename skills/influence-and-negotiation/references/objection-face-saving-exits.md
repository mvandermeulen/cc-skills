# Face-saving exits

Most negotiations don't fail on substance — they fail on face. The buyer needs a way to change position without admitting they were wrong. Build the exit into your move.

Templates:

- _"It's possible the team that gave you that number was working from older information."_ (Bad-faith exit.)
- _"Things change between proposal and close — totally normal that the criteria evolved."_ (Scope-creep exit.)
- _"I know X was on the wishlist; if it turns out we don't need it for V1, we can revisit at renewal."_ (Feature-cut exit.)
- _"You raised this concern earlier and we've taken it seriously — here's what we changed."_ (Concern-validation exit even when their concern was wrong.)

Don't give the exit when it's not needed — gratuitous face-saving comes across as patronising. Give it when you can see the buyer needs to back down and is looking for a way.
