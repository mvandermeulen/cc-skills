# Common Traps — Extended Reference

Less frequent but consequential traps not covered in the SKILL.md summary.

| #  | Trap                                                                         | Counter                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| -- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1  | Escalation ladder                                                            | Name it: _"We've discussed this twice already; I need to understand who has the final authority so we can have one conversation rather than three."_ Works for procurement and internal management escalation.                                                                              |
| 2  | Fixed-envelope claim ("budget closed", "band ceiling", "we don't have flex") | _"How was that number set?"_ / _"What would unlock movement at the next review?"_ — see [objection-procurement-playbook.md](objection-procurement-playbook.md#the-fixed-budget-claim).                                                                                                      |
| 3  | Internal-pressure self-concession                                            | Your urgency must not exceed the counterparty's. Trade close-by-date / quarter-end / fiscal-year for structural value, never give it.                                                                                                                                                       |
| 4  | Mixing issues                                                                | Park: _"Let's resolve scope, then come back to price."_ One issue per round.                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| 5  | Sympathy collapse                                                            | When the counterparty's emotion is intense, slip into _empathie_ (verbalise without sharing) — never _sympathie_ (share the emotion). Sympathie costs you objectivity in the moment when you most need it — see [tactics.md](tactics.md#the-four-relational-stances).                      |
| 6  | Skipping the back-brief                                                      | Before any agreement, the counterparty reformulates each term in their own words. Catches selective memory, manipulation de clôture, and misunderstanding before they become churn / strikes / counter-offers.                                                                              |
